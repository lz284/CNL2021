Goals:  This class will cover necessary theoretical foundation and state of the art performance evaluation methods for wireless networks. In a nutshell, this course will give you the basis for understanding the radio and infrastructure aspect of the wireless networks.

 

Textbooks References and Additional Readings:

 

1.      Class notes will be distributed

2.      D. Bertsekas and R. Gallager, Data Networks, 2nd ed., Prentice-Hall, 1992.

3.      Harri Holma and Antti Toskala, WCDMA for UMTS - Radio Access for Third Generation Mobile Communications, John Wiley & Sons, 2000.

4.      Theodore S. Rappaport, Wireless Communications Principles & Practice , Prentice-Hall, 1996.

5.      V. Garg and J. Wilkes, Wireless and Personal Communications Systems, Prentice-Hall, 1996.

6.      Selected readings from the IEEE Transactions on Vehicular Technology, IEEE/ACM transactions on Networking, IEEE Journal on Selected Areas in Communications (JSAC), IEEE Transactions on Communications, IEEE Communications Magazine and IEEE Personal Communication Magazine and other technical journals.

7.      William Stallings, Wireless Communications and Networks , Prentice-Hall, 2002.

8.      Additional references and readings will be given in class.

 

Grading:

              There will be homework assignments (possibly requiring a model development on a computer), a mid-term and final exams and a term-paper. The acceptable paper topics and the associated timetable will be discussed in class. It would be preferable if your term paper included writing a software program to model a specific aspect of mobility and teletraffic. They will count towards the grade as follows:

              Homework 5%

              Term-paper 25%

              Mid-term Examination 30%

              Final Examination 40%
